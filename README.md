# Feral Freedom

Feral Freedom is a 2D top-down survival game where players navigate the challenges of life as a stray animal. The game was inspired by a real-life event, [Turkey Passes ‘Massacre Law’ to Remove Stray Dogs From Streets](https://time.com/7005319/turkey-stray-dogs-massacre-law/) and was developed as a CPSC427 course project at UBC by a team of six: Selin Uz *(me)*, Vincent Lee, Shuang Qiu, Nicolas Serrano de la Paz, Cengiz Kagan Kuscu, Siri Phanichkrivalkosil.

The game features pixel art animations, interactive dialogues and encounters with NPCs, AI chase logic with patrols, and resource collection to improve your stats and survive in a challenging world.

## Features
### Gameplay:
* Survival Mechanics: Explore a multi-room map, collect resources, and build up your stats while evading patrols and engaging with NPCs.
* Encounter System: Undertale-inspired quick-time battles featuring:
    * Three NPC attack styles.
    * Three player attacks with unique patterns to trace.
* AI-Driven NPCs: Enemies use state-based AI for patrols and chases.

### Visuals & Sound:
* Pixel Art Animations: Animations for both player and NPCs.
* Dynamic Shadows: Advanced lighting for day/night cycles tied to an in-game clock.
* Audio Feedback: Mood-reflective background music and interaction sounds during encounters.

### Quality of Life:
* Save/Load System: Save progress via JSON files.
* FPS Counter: Toggle on/off with the "F" key to see game performance.
* Pause Menu: Toggle on/off with the "P" to stop the game.
